Executive Summary

The methodology in summary.

The Eternal Wealth Partners methodology is the framework for comprehensive wealth planning for the business owner. The methodology integrates the business and personal dimensions of the owner's financial life across seven analytical pillars. The proprietary claim is the framework: the specific organization of business owner planning into seven coordinated pillars and the deliverable architecture that produces the integrated planning picture.

The methodology is distinct from general personal financial planning in three respects.

01
It treats the business and the personal pictures as one integrated whole, not as adjacent domains. Every pillar runs the planning question through both the business lens and the personal lens simultaneously, with the owner positioned at the intersection.
02
It is structured around the integrated life and exit horizon, not retirement alone. The standard planning arc anticipates the owner's life through and beyond a liquidity event (sale, transition, succession, ESOP, or family transfer), with retirement planning as one downstream output of the integrated plan rather than the central organizing question.
03
It is defined by a deliverable architecture that produces a coherent multi-pillar plan, rather than a collection of discrete planning conversations across years. The deliverable architecture allows the methodology to be run consistently across advisors, time, and client circumstances.

Origin, Development & Scope

Twenty years of field work. One integrated framework.

The methodology was developed by Becky Gustafson over approximately twenty years of practice as a financial advisor and estate and business planning specialist, working with high-net-worth and business owner clients. The framework evolved through direct field work with clients in transition: business owners preparing for exits, families navigating multigenerational wealth, executives with concentrated equity compensation, and surviving spouses inheriting both a personal and business picture.

The methodology was not adapted from a third-party framework. It was developed in response to the recurring failure modes observed when standard personal financial planning was applied to business owner clients: the planning broke when the client's business was concentrated relative to their personal assets, when the cash flow conversation could not be separated from the business's actual financial picture, when the exit horizon dominated the retirement horizon, and when the integration of personal and business protection was the actual analytical work the client needed but the planning software could not run.

The Economic Ownership Calculation

Economic Ownership is an EWP-original Pillar 02 construct that quantifies the owner's effective ownership of the business profit stream after debt service. Legal ownership and economic ownership diverge when the business services debt, because debt providers claim a portion of the profit stream that the legal owner does not control.

E = O × (1 − D / P)

Where: E = effective (economic) ownership percentage. O = owner's stated legal ownership interest (0 to 1). D = annual debt service payments (principal plus interest). P = annual EBITDA.

EBITDA is the pre-debt-service operating cash pool, the same denominator used in debt service coverage ratio analysis in lending. Principal repayment is included with interest because every dollar of EBITDA committed to any debt obligation reduces what the owner effectively controls.

Value of EStatusPlain Language
E ≥ 80%HealthyOwner controls most of the profit stream
50% ≤ E < 80%WatchMeaningfully leveraged; monitor trajectory
0% ≤ E < 50%ConcerningMore than half of profit committed to debt
E < 0%DistressedDebt service exceeds operating profit

Scope limitation: Economic Ownership applies only to the Control guardrail display within the Eternal Wealth Guardrails framework. All other surfaces use traditional legal ownership.

The Margin and Multiplier Sequence

An EWP-original Pillar 05 diagnostic that reads the business's margin profile and value multipliers against NAICS code peer data to establish a grounded informational baseline for the planning conversation. The Sequence is not an operational KPI dashboard. It is a planning-perspective read that surfaces where the business sits relative to its industry peer set.

What it reads: Gross margin, operating margin, EBITDA margin, net margin, all benchmarked against NAICS peer data. Implied valuation multipliers (EBITDA multiples, revenue multiples) benchmarked against NAICS peer transaction data. Trajectory of margins and multipliers across 3 to 5 years.

What it produces: Planning observations expressed as informational signals: above peer, at peer, below peer, with directional notes. The observations route into the planning conversation, the Net-Worth Acceleration Map's business-side levers, and the Four-Dimension Exit Readiness Assessment's Business Readiness dimension. They do not route into an operational improvement plan owned by the wealth advisor.

Cross-Pillar Constructs

The architectural backbone of the integrated planning picture.

The methodology includes a defined set of constructs that operate above any single pillar. These cross-pillar constructs draw inputs from multiple pillars and produce outputs that feed back across the methodology.

EWP-Original · Pillar 02 Output

The Eternal Wealth Snapshot

The integrated balance sheet picture across the business and personal sides of the owner's wealth, at a point in time. The single-page artifact that captures the owner's financial position.

Inputs: Business balance sheet, personal balance sheet, all asset classes, ownership, debt, valuation Outputs to: Pillars 03, 05, 07, Wealth Gap, Net-Worth Acceleration Map Refresh: Quarterly
EWP-Original · Pillar 02 Output

The Eternal Wealth Guardrails

The cash flow and structural boundaries within which the integrated picture remains stable. Tri-axis tracking across Liquidity (distribution strategy, cash reserves, runway), Growth (revenue and EBITDA growth targets, market position), and Control (Economic Ownership, debt service limits, ownership maintenance).

Any pillar's recommendation that breaches a Guardrail triggers re-derivation. The Guardrails are the sustainability test for the entire methodology.

Inputs: Cash flow model, balance sheet ratios, debt schedule, Economic Ownership, distribution policy Outputs to: Every pillar (sustainability test) Refresh: Quarterly. Breach is a defined re-derivation trigger.
EWP-Original · Pillar 01 Output

The 18-24 Month Purpose Statement

The actionable middle ground between Anchor Values and Quarterly Action Steps. A multi-component statement covering the next 18 to 24 months using five components: specific intent, lifestyle frame, what gets protected, what gets advanced, and anchor values in service. The Owner Strategy Statement is the team-facing version.

Inputs: Pillar 01 Anchor Values, Multi-Pillar Vision, obstacles, resources Outputs to: Quarterly Action Steps, every pillar (direction-setting), Owner Strategy Statement, Team Alignment Refresh: Reviewed quarterly. Renewed every 18-24 months.
EWP-Original · Cross-Pillar

The Net-Worth Acceleration Map

The cross-pillar sequenced plan that closes the gap between the owner's current accumulated wealth and the wealth required to fund the post-exit life and legacy obligations. The Map operates simultaneously across two integrated levers: the owner-side compensation and accumulation lever (Pillars 02 and 04) and the business-side value lever (Pillars 02 and 05). The integration of the two levers is the construct's distinguishing feature.

Inputs: Wealth Gap, valuation, Margin and Multiplier Sequence, Economic Ownership, cash flow, tax strategy, compensation Outputs to: Post-Exit Wealth Projection, Quarterly Action Steps, each pillar where a lever is pulled Refresh: Quarterly. Re-derived on major input changes.
EWP-Original · Cross-Pillar

The Post-Exit Wealth Projection

The forward projection of the owner's total wealth position once business exit proceeds net of tax are included, modeled at projected value and timing. Not a gap calculation but a scenario projection. It sits alongside the Wealth Gap and does the opposite job: the Wealth Gap is a conservative anchor that excludes exit proceeds; the Post-Exit Wealth Projection includes them, at projected value and timing.

Inputs: Personal accumulation, business exit proceeds net of tax, post-exit lifestyle, legacy obligations Outputs to: Four-Dimension Exit Readiness (Financial), Net-Worth Acceleration Map, Plan Success Stress-Test Refresh: Quarterly during active exit planning. Annually otherwise.
EWP-Original · Cross-Pillar

The Plan Success Stress-Test

The cross-pillar simulation engine that produces the Probability of Plan Success. The engine integrates three inputs traditionally evaluated separately: the financial picture (from Pillar 02 and the Wealth Gap), the personal-emotional-mental readiness (from Pillar 01 outputs), and the business readiness including sellability (from Pillar 05). The integration of these three readiness dimensions in a single stress-tested output is the construct's distinguishing feature.

The headline output is the Probability of Plan Success, a distributional statistic produced across plausible alternative histories. Available continuously and re-run for each plan iteration.

Outputs to: Four-Dimension Exit Readiness (Financial), Net-Worth Acceleration Map, Pillar 01 Purpose Statement renewal Refresh: Continuously available. Recalibrated on input changes.
EWP-Original · Pillar 06 Output

The Four-Dimension Exit Readiness Assessment

The structured evaluation of the owner's readiness to exit, scored across four dimensions — anchored on the two owner-side reads: whether the owner is financially ready to fund the life after the business, and whether the owner is personally ready to leave it.

Dimension 1. Financial Readiness. Does the owner have the integrated financial picture to fund the post-exit life and legacy? Three readings: Wealth Gap (EPI), Post-Exit Wealth Projection (EWP), Plan Success Stress-Test (EWP).

Dimension 2. Business Readiness. Is the business transferable, sellable, and at what value, on what timeline? Evaluated from the planning perspective.

Dimension 3. Personal-Emotional-Mental Readiness. The EWP-distinctive piece. Evaluated against Pillar 01 outputs: do the Anchor Values find expression in the post-exit life, does the Vision align with a life after the business, is the owner's identity sufficiently distinct from the business.

Dimension 4. Team and Coordination Readiness. Is the professional team aligned around the Owner Strategy Statement? Are the right specialists in place?

Outputs to: Exit roadmap, Net-Worth Acceleration Map, Quarterly Action Steps, Multi-Pro coordination Refresh: Annually minimum. Quarterly approaching exit. Event-driven.

The Wealth-Advisor-Perspective Discipline

Observe. Diagnose. Inform. Route.

The methodology operates from a defined professional posture: the wealth advisor observes, diagnoses, informs, and routes. The advisor does not perform specialist work in tax, legal, operational, or transaction execution domains.

Observe

The advisor reads the integrated business and personal picture across all seven pillars. The reading is comprehensive (every pillar) and continuous (refreshed on the defined cadence).

Diagnose

The advisor surfaces what the reading reveals. Gaps, opportunities, sustainability concerns, alignment issues, and trigger dates are made visible as planning artifacts.

Inform

The advisor presents diagnostic outputs to the owner and to relevant specialists in the form each audience needs. The owner sees the integrated picture; specialists see the inputs for their work.

Route

The advisor routes specialist work to the appropriate professionals through structural checkpoints specified in each pillar. Specialist outputs flow back through the system.

What the advisor does not do.

  • Provide tax advice. Surfacing tax-planning implications is methodology work. Providing tax advice is the CPA's work.
  • Draft legal documents. Reading documents for planning implications is methodology work. Drafting is the attorney's work.
  • Direct operational improvements at the business. Reading the economic position and surfacing observations is methodology work. Directing change is the owner and operating team's work.
  • Score the business against operator-level value driver or intangible capital frameworks. These are operational disciplines outside the wealth advisor channel.
  • Represent the owner in a transaction. Coordinating across the deal team is methodology work. Negotiating the deal is the M&A advisor's and attorney's work.
  • Serve as fiduciary trustee, executor, or family office administrator unless explicitly engaged in a separate professional capacity.
Why the Discipline Matters

Regulatory integrity. The methodology operates within the regulatory boundaries of the wealth advisor channel by design. Structural checkpoints route specialist work to professionals who hold the relevant licenses.

Professional respect across the team. The advisor does not encroach on the CPA's, attorney's, or operating team's territory. The team operates from common direction without role conflict.

Methodology coherence. The advisor's value comes from integration, not from specialist substitution. Holding the advisor in the advisor role protects the integration function the methodology is built around.

Exit Team Alignment (ETA)

The coordination layer.

Exit Team Alignment is the named coordination layer of the methodology. It describes the work by which the wealth advisor coordinates across the owner's professional team using the methodology's structural features. The methodology is exit-horizon-anchored: every pillar's work is oriented to the owner's eventual transition, regardless of how far off that transition is. The acronym ETA also carries the time-bound meaning of estimated time of arrival, reinforcing that the work is a continuous read against a planned destination.

The Owner Strategy Statement
Pillar 01 deliverable. The team-facing articulation of the 18-24 Month Purpose Statement. The document the team aligns around.
The Team Alignment Meeting
Pillar 01 cadence. Annual or more-frequent working session that brings the professional team into common direction. Structured agenda: opening, values check, pillar coordination, calendar alignment, open issues, cadence setting.
The Affirmation Report
Pillar 01 deliverable. Ties every recommendation across all seven pillars back to the Anchor Values and the vision. The team uses it to validate alignment of any new recommendation.
The Routing Language
The structural checkpoints in each pillar that name what the advisor does and what gets routed to which specialist.
The Multi-Pro Coordination Plan
Pillar 06 deliverable. Sequences the work of the M&A advisor, attorney, valuation specialist, CPA, wealth advisor, and family governance counsel through the exit transaction.
The Tax Strategy Memo & Legal Coordination Brief

The wealth advisor is not the team's hierarchical lead. The advisor is the team's coordinating center. Each specialist owns their domain. The advisor owns the integration. ETA is a structural feature available to advisors who take it on; the Seven Pillars run and the planning picture stays integrated whether or not ETA is fully active.

Integration Logic Across Pillars

One methodology, one data picture.

The methodology operates as an integrated framework, not as seven separate planning exercises. The following principles govern how inputs and outputs flow between pillars and cross-pillar constructs.

One methodology, one data picture.

All seven pillars and the cross-pillar constructs operate against a single shared data picture of the client. Updates propagate to every construct that depends on the changed input.

Sustainability is tested across pillars, not within them.

No pillar's recommendation is considered final until it has been tested against the Eternal Wealth Guardrails in Pillar 02. A recommendation that breaks the integrated cash flow picture is not a viable recommendation.

Pillar 01 is the criterion.

Every recommendation from every pillar is evaluated against the Anchor Values, the Multi-Pillar Vision, and the 18-24 Month Purpose Statement. Recommendations that conflict with the owner's stated direction are flagged and revised.

Tax efficiency runs through every pillar.

Pillar 04 is not a sequential step. It is a coordinative function that touches every other pillar's recommendations. Every pillar's outputs are evaluated for tax-efficient implementation.

The cross-pillar constructs are the integrating outputs.

The constructs specified in Section 6 are how the methodology produces integrated answers rather than parallel pillar outputs. Each construct draws from multiple pillars and routes back to multiple pillars.

Bidirectional flow between adjacent pillars.

Pillar 02 and Pillar 05 share data continuously. Similar bidirectional flows operate between Pillars 02 and 04 (cash flow and tax), Pillars 03 and 07 (risk and legacy), and Pillars 05 and 06 (valuation and exit).

The Recalibration Loop

The methodology is not a one-time analysis. Each pillar refreshes on its defined cadence. When any pillar's inputs change materially, the dependency map drives propagation: downstream pillars are reviewed, cross-pillar constructs are recalculated, and the Plan Success Stress-Test is re-run.

The recalibration loop is what allows the methodology to remain accurate across a multi-decade arc. It is also what makes the methodology operationally complex: running seven pillars on continuous refresh cadence is the analytical work that the Contnuum platform substrate is designed to support.
